Kanjeevaram Saree

Silk Fabric: Kanjeevaram sarees are woven using pure mulberry silk threads, which are known for their durability, luster, and smooth texture.

Zari Work:The zari work adds a luxurious and regal look to the saree.

Contrast Borders: Kanjeevaram sarees are characterized by their bold and contrasting borders, often featuring intricate designs and patterns.

Nauvari Saree from Maharashtra

Length: As the name suggests, a Nauvari saree measures nine yards in length, which is significantly longer than the standard six-yard saree.

Blouse: Nauvari sarees are typically paired with a short-sleeved, fitted blouse, which complements the overall look. The blouse may have traditional or contemporary designs, depending on the occasion.

Fabric and Design: Nauvari sarees are often made of traditional fabrics like cotton or silk, with rich colors and intricate designs.

Bandhani Saree

Patterns and Designs: The tie-dye patterns on Bandhani sarees can vary from tiny dots (known as "khombi" or "bindi") to more complex geometric and floral designs.

Bright and Vibrant Colors: Bandhani sarees are known for their vibrant and bold color combinations. They often feature a range of vivid colors, including red, green, blue, yellow, and more.

Traditional Craftsmanship: Bandhani is a traditional art form that has been practiced for centuries. It requires skilled artisans who have mastered the techniques of tying and dyeing to create intricate and symmetrical patterns.

Tant Saree from West Bengal

Lightweight and Breathable: Tant sarees are known for their lightweight and breathable nature, making them comfortable to wear in warm weather.

Cotton Fabric: Tant sarees are primarily made from cotton, which is widely grown in West Bengal. The use of cotton makes these sarees soft and comfortable, perfect for daily wear.

Wide Range of Colors and Designs: Tant sarees come in a wide variety of colors and designs. They often feature simple and elegant motifs, including traditional patterns like checks, stripes, and floral designs.
